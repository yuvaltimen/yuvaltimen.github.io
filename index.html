About Me

I'm Backend Engineer who focuses on Data Engineering, AI, System Design, and DevOps.

My interest in computers first started while I was taking a course on Mathematical Cryptography during my undergraduate studies. We were given an assignment which involved running the binary computations for the entire 10 rounds of the AES encryption algorithm. I remember chugging through about 12 pages of notebook paper, tediously writing out the 1's and the 0's, doing the binary addition, my head swirling with digits, and worrying that I had missed a single carry operation which would render my 3 days of work useless. It was then that the first time I'd ever had the thought: "There's gotta be a way to automate this." That one thought was the beginning of my career.

My first job straight out of college was as a Backend Engineer for Gryphon.ai, a Sales Technology company based in Boston. Here, I worked with Google's Dialogflow module, as well as our Java Websocket application and Kafka, in order to integrate realtime capabilities into sales calls through Zoom. I worked with our machine learning team to improve model accuracy, reduce training time, and implement an active learning pipeline for constant retraining.

Then I moved to KAYAK, where I worked as a Data Engineer. Here, I developed and optimized large-scale data pipelines, designed, deployed and documented API's, and implemented a few AI/ML solutions. I wrote algorithms for geospatial lookup, implemented geospatial indices for efficient radius lookups, and improved our production indexing time by >96%. I've worked with a variety of ETL tools, databases (both relational and NoSQL), frameworks, languages, build tools, and more.
